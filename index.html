About Me

I am a dedicated computer science researcher and aspiring graduate student with a passion for algorithms, privacy and cryptography, bioinformatics, and deep learning. I hold a degree in Mathematics and Philosophy from Yale University and have completed extensive coursework at Princeton and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

My current home institution is the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute (DFCI) at Harvard Medical School (HMS), where I focus on deep learning-based methods development for computational pathology under Bill Lotter and Eliezer Van Allen. I am also affiliated with the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard and New York Genome Center (NYGC).

My ultimate goal is to pursue a Ph.D. in computer science or applied mathematics. In my spare time, I enjoy solo-traveling, combat sports, and language-learning.
